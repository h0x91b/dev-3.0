Added narrow-viewport carousel navigation for the Kanban board (Level 1). On a sub-768px viewport (phone via dev3 remote, or a narrow browser window — gated by a media query) the board shows one column at a time: swipe sideways or use the pager chevrons/dots to change column, scroll vertically to browse its tasks. Collapsed columns are excluded; empty columns are kept. Browser remote now renders at device-width so the layout is responsive. Hover terminal previews are disabled on touch and narrow viewports (no hover there, and the popover just covers the card). The carousel was also generalised into a product-wide narrow-viewport (mobile) UX doctrine in docs/ux/ (bible §12, ux-architecture responsive block, a UX decision, and feature-plans/narrow-viewport-doctrine.md) — one-element-at-a-time per surface, a reconciled 768px breakpoint, a swipe gesture law, a touch navigation/action-reachability model, and a mandated BottomSheet primitive. Full UX plan and the terminal pane carousel (Level 2, next step) are documented in docs/ux/feature-plans/mobile-carousel-navigation.md. Idea by Ittai Zeidman.
